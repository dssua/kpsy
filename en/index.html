Areas of Focus

Depression

Everyone feels depressed at some point during life, however for people with depression, these feelings are much more intense and do not simply go away or get better. Depression is a serious illness which is caused by changes in brain chemistry. These changes decrease the brain’s ability to send and receive neurotransmitters and chemicals. Which can cause problems with circulating the chemicals in the brain tied to positive emotions.

Several factors can influence whether or not a person develops depression:

  • Heredity
  • Medical conditions
  • Difficult circumstances
  • Changes in hormone levels
  • Stress
  • Grief

I am here to provide the best type of treatment for each person’s unique struggles with depression.

Anxiety and Panic attacks

While many people experience anxiety at some point in life, those with an anxiety disorder feel anxious or worried all of the time. It is also much stronger than for most people. These feelings can get so bad that they cause problems with your daily activities, work and relationships.

I offer Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and mindfulness-based cognitive therapy for anxiety disorders. CBT is recommended by The National Institute of Mental Health for anxiety disorders treatment.
