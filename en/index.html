Areas of Focus

Depression

Everyone feels depressed at some point during life, however for people with depression, these feelings are much more intense and do not simply go away or get better. It is a serious illness which is caused by changes in brain chemistry. These changes decrease the brain’s ability to send and receive neurotransmitters and chemicals. Which can cause problems with circulating the chemicals in the brain tied to positive emotions.

Several factors can influence whether or not a person develops depression including:

  • Heredity
  • Medical conditions
  • Difficult circumstances
  • Changes in hormone levels
  • Stress
  • Grief

Many people often go without the help they need. I am here to provide the best type of treatment for each person’s unique struggles with depression.

Anxiety, Phobias, Panic attacks

While many people experience anxiety at some point in life, those with an anxiety disorder feel anxious or worried all of the time. It is also much stronger than for most people. These feelings can get so bad that people get in the way of performing daily activities, going to work or school, and can damage relationships.

My approach to therapy is integrative. This means that the exact treatment used will be based on the person’s unique needs, how serious the anxiety is, and how the person is responding to various forms of therapy.
